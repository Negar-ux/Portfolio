Inclusive Innovation Design Challenge Workshops

During my time at the School of Engineering Practice and Technology (SEPT), I co-designed and co-facilitated three inclusive design thinking workshops for engineering students.

Role: Workshop Co-Designer & Co-Facilitator Context: School of Engineering Practice and Technology (SEPT) Focus: Workshop Design & Facilitation

Project Overview

During my time at SEPT, I collaborated on designing and facilitating three inclusive design thinking workshops specifically for engineering students. The workshops were designed to help participants engage in interactive design exercises to become more aware of and engaged with EDIA (Equity, Diversity, Inclusion, and Accessibility) principles.

Workshop Design Methodology

Design Thinking Integration

Introduced participants to Design Thinking, Co-Design, and Liberatory Design - an extension of design thinking that focuses on breaking habits contributing to inequity in design work.

Interactive Exercises

Developed engaging design exercises including Critical Alphabet and Design Challenge activities for co-design.

Template Redesign

Redesigned worksheet templates like 'Build Your Character' and 'Character's Journey' to make them more accessible and easier to follow during workshops.

Workshop Deliverables

Workshop 1: Inclusive Design Workshop

Helped participants engage with EDIA principles through Design Thinking, Co-Design, and Liberatory Design methodologies.

Character Development Exercise

Participants developed fictional personas based on their experiences and mapped character journeys considering intersectional identity factors.

Virtual Follow-Up Workshop

'Cultivating Trust and Creative Confidence' - drew inspiration from research on engineering values and their impact on engineering culture.

Conference Presentation

Contributed to conference papers and presentations sharing the workshop methodology and outcomes.

Impact & Learning

The workshops successfully introduced engineering students to inclusive design principles through human-centered design and co-design methodologies. The project resulted in conference papers and presentations, contributing to the academic discourse on inclusive design education.
