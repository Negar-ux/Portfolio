Hospital Discharge Journey Tool

A collaborative and interactive tool to help patients, family members, and hospital staff with discharge planning.

Role: UX Researcher Duration: 8 months Team: Project Supervisor, UX Researcher, Research Student

Project Overview

This project took place at St. Joseph Healthcare Hamilton Hospital, Canada, focusing on improving the hospital discharge process for Alternate Level of Care (ALC) patients.

Key Challenge

The communication gap between patients, families, and hospital staff was one of the barriers to a smooth hospital discharge journey. This gap affected patient flow in the hospital and the overall experience of patients and family members.

Key Statistics: ALC accounts for 14% of Canadian hospital days and utilizes up to 7,500 acute care beds annually, making this a critical healthcare challenge.

Research Methodology

Initial Research

Conducted comprehensive literature review to identify communication gaps in the discharge process and understand the broader context of ALC patient challenges.

User Interviews

Conducted 25 in-depth interviews with key stakeholders including social workers, discharge coordinators, family members, and patient advisors.

Stakeholder Mapping

Created comprehensive stakeholder maps to understand the complex relationships and communication flows in the discharge process.

Design Process

Stakeholder Mapping

Created detailed stakeholder maps to understand the complex relationships and communication flows in the discharge process.

Journey Mapping

Developed comprehensive journey maps to visualize the entire discharge experience from multiple perspectives.

Interactive Prototype

Designed an interactive collaborative tool focused on improving communication and coordination.

Style Guide

Created a style guide considering the needs of older adult users and accessibility requirements.

Design Features

Care Team Communication

Help care team initiate difficult conversations with patients and families.

Patient & Family Engagement

Engage patients and families actively in discharge planning meetings.

Clear System Information

Provide clear and accessible information about hospital systems and processes.

Outcomes & Key Learnings

The project resulted in an interactive prototype for discharge planning that improved communication between patients, families, and hospital staff. The solution addressed the critical communication gaps identified in our research.

Key Reflections

Documentation Importance

Learned the critical importance of documenting observations throughout the research process.

Design Bias Awareness

Developed awareness of design bias and the importance of user-centered approaches in healthcare.

Rapid Prototyping Value

Discovered the value of rapid prototyping for testing ideas quickly with stakeholders.
